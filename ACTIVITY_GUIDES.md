# Research Navigator - Detailed Activity Guides

## Stage 1: Domain Knowledge Discovery (2-4 weeks)

### 🎯 Learning Objective
Transform from "I don't know what to research" to "I'm passionate about [specific domain]"

### 🎭 Activity: Domain Dating Workshop (90 minutes)

#### Pre-Activity Assessment (10 min)
**Question**: "How do you currently feel about choosing a research domain?"
- A: Completely lost, no idea where to start 😰
- B: Have vague interests, not sure if they're research-worthy 🤔
- C: Know 1-2 domains, want to explore more 📚
- D: Clear about my domain, ready to dive deep 🎯

#### Orientation (30 min)

**What is Domain Knowledge Discovery?**
- It's like dating before marriage - explore before committing!
- Research domains are vast: AI, IoT, Healthcare, Energy, Education, etc.
- Your domain should excite you for 2-5 years minimum

**Why Does It Matter?**
- Wrong domain = miserable research journey
- Right domain = you'll work weekends and love it!
- Passion fuels persistence through challenges

**✅ What TO DO:**
1. Explore 5 different domains (not just 1)
2. Read 2-3 recent papers in each domain
3. Talk to researchers in those domains
4. Identify problems that bug you personally
5. Check funding availability in that domain

**❌ What NOT to DO:**
1. Don't pick based on "what's hot" or trending
2. Don't choose because your friend chose it
3. Don't select based on perceived "easiness"
4. Don't commit without exploring alternatives
5. Don't ignore your natural curiosity

**Success Story**
> "I thought I'd do AI because everyone was doing it. Hated it. Then I explored healthcare tech - found my calling! Now I have 5 papers and love my research!" - Dr. Priya, REVA University

#### Hands-On Activity (60 min)

**Round 1: Speed Dating with Domains (30 min)**

Setup: 5 stations, each representing a domain
- Station 1: Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
- Station 2: Internet of Things & Embedded Systems
- Station 3: Healthcare & Biotechnology
- Station 4: Sustainable Energy & Environment
- Station 5: Education Technology & Social Innovation

Each station has:
- 3 recent breakthrough papers (abstracts)
- 5 unsolved problems in that domain
- Video of researcher talking about their work (3 min)
- Hands-on demo/simulation

Participants spend 5 minutes at each station:
- Minute 1-2: Watch video
- Minute 3-4: Try demo/read abstracts
- Minute 5: Rate excitement level (1-10)

**Round 2: Deep Dive (20 min)**

Participants choose top 2 domains and:
1. Read one full paper (skim method)
2. Identify 3 interesting problems
3. Imagine solving one problem - how would it feel?
4. Discuss with someone who chose same domain

**Round 3: Commitment Check (10 min)**

Answer these questions:
1. Which domain made you most excited? Why?
2. What problem in that domain bugs you personally?
3. Can you see yourself working on this for 2 years?
4. What's your next step to learn more?

#### Reflection & Sharing (20 min)

**Group Sharing** (15 min)
- 5-6 volunteers share their domain choice and why
- Others ask questions
- Facilitator highlights diverse choices

**Key Takeaways** (5 min)
- It's okay to change domains later (but try to commit for now)
- Passion > Trend
- Your curiosity is your compass
- Multiple domains can intersect (interdisciplinary research)

#### Post-Activity Assessment (10 min)

**Question**: "What will you DO THIS WEEK?"
- A: Maybe think about domains... 🤷
- B: Read 5 papers in my chosen domain 📖
- C: Talk to 2 researchers in my domain 💬
- D: Identify 3 specific problems to explore 🎯

**Action Plan Template**
```
My chosen domain: _______________
Why I chose it: _______________
3 problems I find interesting:
1. _______________
2. _______________
3. _______________

This week I will:
- [ ] Read ___ papers
- [ ] Talk to ___ researchers
- [ ] Explore ___ problem deeply

Accountability partner: _______________
Check-in date: _______________
```

## Stage 2: Research Article Reading (4-6 weeks)

### 🎯 Learning Objective
Transform from "Papers are boring and confusing" to "I can critically analyze research papers"

### 🎭 Activity: Paper Reading Marathon (2 hours)

#### Pre-Activity Assessment (10 min)
**Question**: "What's your current relationship with research papers?"
- A: Never read a full paper, too intimidating 😰
- B: Read abstracts, skip the rest 🤷
- C: Read papers but don't understand everything 📚
- D: Read papers critically, take notes 📝

#### Orientation (30 min)

**What is Effective Paper Reading?**
- It's NOT reading every word like a novel
- It's strategic reading: Abstract → Figures → Intro → Conclusion → Method
- It's active reading: questioning, noting, connecting

**Why Does It Matter?**
- Papers are your research GPS - they show what's been done
- Critical reading reveals gaps (your research opportunities)
- Good readers become good writers

**✅ What TO DO:**
1. Use the "3-Pass Method"
   - Pass 1: Skim (5 min) - get the gist
   - Pass 2: Read (20 min) - understand main points
   - Pass 3: Deep dive (60 min) - understand everything
2. Take structured notes (not random highlights)
3. Create a paper summary template
4. Read 20 papers in your domain (4 per week)
5. Discuss papers with peers

**❌ What NOT to DO:**
1. Don't hoard 100 papers without reading
2. Don't read linearly from start to finish
3. Don't highlight everything (= highlighting nothing)
4. Don't read alone - discuss with others
5. Don't skip the methodology section

**Success Story**
> "I used to download 50 papers and read none. Then I committed to 3 papers/week with notes. In 2 months, I identified my research gap!" - Rahul, PhD Scholar

#### Hands-On Activity (60 min)

**Round 1: Paper Bingo (20 min)**

Give everyone the same paper. Create a bingo card with:
```
[Novel Contribution] [Research Gap] [Methodology]
[Key Finding]        [Limitation]   [Future Work]
[Dataset Used]       [Baseline]     [Evaluation Metric]
```

First to find all 9 elements wins!

**Round 2: Critical Reading Challenge (25 min)**

In groups of 3, read different papers and answer:
1. What problem does this paper solve?
2. How do they solve it? (in simple words)
3. What are the results? (numbers/graphs)
4. What's missing? (what didn't they do?)
5. What would you do differently?

Each group presents their paper in 3 minutes.

**Round 3: Gap Matrix Creation (15 min)**

Create a table:
```
| Paper | Problem | Solution | Gap Identified |
|-------|---------|----------|----------------|
| 1     |         |          |                |
| 2     |         |          |                |
| 3     |         |          |                |
```

This becomes your research gap database!

#### Reflection & Sharing (20 min)

**Aha Moments** (10 min)
- What surprised you about papers?
- What's easier than you thought?
- What's still confusing?

**Reading Strategies** (10 min)
- Share your note-taking method
- What tools do you use? (Mendeley, Zotero, Notion)
- How do you organize papers?

#### Post-Activity Assessment (10 min)

**Question**: "What will you DO THIS MONTH?"
- A: Download some papers... 📥
- B: Read 10 papers with basic notes 📝
- C: Read 20 papers with gap matrix 📊
- D: Read 30 papers + discuss with peers weekly 🎯

**Reading Plan Template**
```
Goal: Read ___ papers in ___ weeks
Papers per week: ___
Reading schedule: ___ (days/times)

Paper tracking sheet:
Week 1: Papers 1-5
Week 2: Papers 6-10
Week 3: Papers 11-15
Week 4: Papers 16-20

Discussion group: _______________
Meeting schedule: _______________
```

## Stage 3: Research Gap Identification (3-4 weeks)

### 🎯 Learning Objective
Transform from "Everything has been done" to "I found 5 research gaps!"

### 🎭 Activity: Gap Hunting Challenge (90 minutes)

#### Pre-Activity Assessment (10 min)
**Question**: "How do you currently identify research gaps?"
- A: No idea what a research gap is 😰
- B: I see gaps but not sure if they're valid 🤔
- C: I can identify gaps from papers 📚
- D: I have a validated gap ready to work on 🎯

#### Orientation (30 min)

**What is a Research Gap?**
- It's NOT just "no one has done this"
- It's a meaningful problem that needs solving
- Types of gaps:
  1. Knowledge gap (we don't know X)
  2. Methodological gap (current methods don't work for Y)
  3. Application gap (theory exists but not applied to Z)
  4. Population gap (studied in A but not in B)

**Why Does It Matter?**
- Your gap = your research contribution
- No gap = no novelty = no publication
- Good gap = fundable research

**✅ What TO DO:**
1. Read "Future Work" sections in papers
2. Look for contradictory results across papers
3. Identify limitations in current methods
4. Find problems mentioned but not solved
5. Validate gap with supervisor/experts

**❌ What NOT to DO:**
1. Don't force a gap that doesn't exist
2. Don't pick a gap just because it's easy
3. Don't ignore if someone already solved it
4. Don't choose a gap without checking feasibility
5. Don't work on a gap without passion

**Success Story**
> "I read 25 papers. Found 10 gaps. Discussed with my guide. 3 were already solved, 4 were too hard, 2 were unfeasible. But 1 was perfect - that became my PhD!" - Sneha, PhD Scholar

#### Hands-On Activity (60 min)

**Round 1: Gap Detective Game (20 min)**

Give 5 paper abstracts. For each, identify:
- What did they do?
- What did they NOT do?
- What do they suggest for future work?
- What limitations do they mention?

Create a "Gap Catalog" with all findings.

**Round 2: Gap Validation Workshop (25 min)**

For each identified gap, answer:
1. **Is it novel?** (Has anyone solved this?)
2. **Is it important?** (Does it matter?)
3. **Is it feasible?** (Can you do it with available resources?)
4. **Is it interesting?** (Are you excited about it?)

Score each gap: 4 Yes = Excellent, 3 Yes = Good, 2 Yes = Maybe, 1 Yes = Skip

**Round 3: Gap Pitch Competition (15 min)**

Each person pitches their best gap in 2 minutes:
- The problem
- Why it matters
- How you'll solve it
- Expected impact

Peers vote on most compelling gaps.

#### Reflection & Sharing (20 min)

**Gap Gallery Walk** (15 min)
- Post all identified gaps on wall
- Everyone walks around, adds comments
- Identify common themes
- Spot potential collaborations

**Expert Feedback** (5 min)
- Facilitator provides feedback on top 5 gaps
- Discusses validation criteria
- Shares how to refine gaps

#### Post-Activity Assessment (10 min)

**Question**: "What will you DO THIS WEEK?"
- A: Think about possible gaps... 🤔
- B: List 5 potential gaps 📝
- C: Validate 3 gaps with supervisor 💬
- D: Choose 1 gap and start working! 🎯

**Gap Validation Template**
```
Gap Title: _______________

Problem Statement:
_______________

Why it's a gap:
- Not addressed in papers: _______________
- Mentioned as future work in: _______________
- Limitation in current approaches: _______________

Validation:
✓ Novel? Yes/No - Evidence: _______________
✓ Important? Yes/No - Impact: _______________
✓ Feasible? Yes/No - Resources: _______________
✓ Interesting? Yes/No - Passion level: ___/10

Supervisor feedback: _______________
Decision: Proceed / Refine / Abandon
```

## Facilitator Guide

### Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Print activity materials
- [ ] Set up stations/groups
- [ ] Test videos/demos
- [ ] Prepare examples
- [ ] Arrange prizes for competitions
- [ ] Create assessment forms
- [ ] Set up collaboration tools

### During Activity
- [ ] Start with energy and enthusiasm
- [ ] Use humor and analogies
- [ ] Encourage participation
- [ ] Manage time strictly
- [ ] Provide individual attention
- [ ] Celebrate small wins
- [ ] Take photos/videos for documentation

### After Activity
- [ ] Collect assessments
- [ ] Share resources
- [ ] Follow up with participants
- [ ] Track action plan completion
- [ ] Gather feedback for improvement
- [ ] Document success stories

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge**: Participants are shy/not participating
**Solution**: Start with pair work, then small groups, then large group

**Challenge**: Activity running over time
**Solution**: Have a "fast-forward" version ready

**Challenge**: Some participants finish early
**Solution**: Have extension activities prepared

**Challenge**: Technical issues with demos
**Solution**: Have backup paper-based activities

**Challenge**: Participants don't see value
**Solution**: Share real success stories and outcomes
