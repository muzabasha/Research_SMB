# 🎓 Research Navigator - Vision & Purpose

## 🎯 Core Mission

**To empower students and faculty with comprehensive guidance and awareness about research, enabling them to embark on their research journey from zero to impactful outcomes.**

---

## 🌟 Vision Statement

Research Navigator is designed to democratize research education by providing:

1. **Awareness**: Understanding what research truly means
2. **Guidance**: Step-by-step direction through the research process
3. **Education**: Learning research methodologies and best practices
4. **Empowerment**: Building confidence to start and complete research
5. **Impact**: Creating meaningful contributions to knowledge

---

## 👥 Target Audience

### Students (Primary Focus)
- **Undergraduate students** exploring research for the first time

### Faculty (Secondary Focus)
- **New faculty members** beginning their research careers

- **Faculty transitioning** to research-focused roles

## 🛤️ Research Journey Stages

### Stage 1: Awareness (Zero Knowledge)
**Goal**: Understand what research is and why it matters

**Content**:
- Introduction to research
- Types of research
- Research career paths
- Success stories
- Common misconceptions

**Outcome**: Student understands research and feels motivated to start

---

### Stage 2: Exploration (Beginner)
**Goal**: Identify interests and potential research areas

**Content**:
- Domain exploration
- Interest assessment
- Skill evaluation
- Resource availability
- Mentor identification

**Outcome**: Student has identified a research domain of interest

---

### Stage 3: Problem Identification (Novice)
**Goal**: Define a clear, researchable problem

**Content**:
- Problem identification techniques
- Research gap analysis
- Problem statement formulation
- Feasibility assessment
- Scope definition

**Outcome**: Student has a well-defined research problem

---

### Stage 4: Literature Review (Developing)
**Goal**: Understand existing research and identify gaps

**Content**:
- Literature search strategies
- Database navigation
- Critical reading techniques
- Note-taking methods
- Citation management
- Gap identification

**Outcome**: Student understands the research landscape

---

### Stage 5: Methodology Design (Intermediate)
**Goal**: Plan how to conduct the research

**Content**:
- Research design types
- Variable identification
- Hypothesis formulation
- Data collection methods
- Analysis techniques
- Ethical considerations

**Outcome**: Student has a complete research methodology

---

### Stage 6: Outcome Planning (Advanced)
**Goal**: Define expected results and impact

**Content**:
- Outcome prediction
- Impact assessment
- Timeline planning
- Resource planning
- Risk management
- Success metrics

**Outcome**: Student has a complete research plan

---

### Stage 7: Execution (Practitioner)
**Goal**: Conduct the research (Future enhancement)

**Content**:
- Data collection
- Analysis execution
- Result interpretation
- Troubleshooting
- Progress tracking

---

### Stage 8: Dissemination (Expert)
**Goal**: Share research findings (Future enhancement)

**Content**:
- Paper writing
- Conference presentations
- Publication process
- Patent filing
- Industry collaboration
